San Francisco Bay Area Bike-Sharing¶

by Ahmed Hamdan¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the distribution of using bike-share system over 24/7 and the most age group using bike share system. The main focus were on: duration in seconds, member birth year, gender, start day and hour.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consisted of trip and user details for each bike using at Feb 2019 approximately 175000 trip. The attributes included the start and end (time and station) details, as well as user (gender, birth year, subscription) details.

  • We removed trips with missing data (8462) trip
  • Since the main idea of bike share is using bike to move inside city fast from point to point not keeping bike with customer for long time or next day, so we select the max period of bike usage is 45 minutes and remove dataset which exceed that limit (2306) trip
  • Also we removed we remove all trips for subscribers above 80 old as we suspect that it's false data as bike is not safe for aging people (203) trip

Distribution of Bike Using Duration¶

Duration of using bikes in the dataset has a very large range of values, from about 1 Minute at the lowest, to about 45 Minutes at the highest. Plotted on a logarithmic scale, the distribution of duration takes a unimodal shape with peak at about 9 minutes.

Age and Bikes¶

People between 20 and 40 years old are most users for bike share and spend wide range of durations using bikes from 5 minutes till 45 minutes but the maximum duration is below 15 minutes, as the members getting older the spent less durations and fewer number of trips
